## The Hidden Cost of AI Tool Sprawl

In the past 18 months, I've watched engineering teams go from using 2-3 AI tools to maintaining subscriptions for 7, 12, even 15 different services. ChatGPT, GitHub Copilot, Midjourney, Anthropic Claude, Replit, Cursor, and a dozen others have become essential parts of the modern development workflow.

But here's what no one talks about: **these tools add up fast**. A team of 10 developers might be spending $2,500+ per month on AI subscriptions alone. And with enterprise pricing tiers, vendor lock-in, and overlapping features, it's easy to overpay by 30-50% or more.

## The Problem: No Visibility Into AI Spend

Most engineering leaders I talk to have zero visibility into their AI tool spend. They know they're paying for Copilot and maybe a few other tools, but they don't know:

- How much they're actually spending per developer
- Whether they're getting the best pricing tier
- If they have redundant tools doing the same job
- How their spend compares to similar companies

This lack of visibility leads to three big problems:

1. **Overpaying for unused features** - Enterprise plans with features your team never uses
2. **Tool redundancy** - Multiple tools solving the same problem
3. **Missed optimization opportunities** - Better pricing tiers or alternative tools available
